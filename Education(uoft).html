The below are the courses that I have taken during 2019 winter. My cumulative GPA(cGPA) by then was 3.97.

  1. CSC165H1 Mathematics Expression and Reasoning for Computer Scientists

    2. CSC165H1 Mathematics Expression and Reasoning for Computer Scientists

    Instructed by Professor David Liu

    In this course, I have learnt about major mathematical concepts in Computer Science. I got a chance to explore Proof By Induction, Big O notation, graphs and Trees and more. Even though the course was challenging, I am glad that I took it, as it is amazing to see how much I was capable to achieved in this short period of time. My final grade for this course is A.

  2. CSC148H1 Introduction to Computer Science

    3. CSC148H1 Introduction to Computer Science

    Instructed by Professor Diane Horton

    After completing the introductory course for Python, I learnt about recursion, trees and linked list in this course. The assignments in this course were pretty fun. I got to work with a partner. We were given two interesting tasks. The first assignment was interesting in terms of its complicated structure. Our goal is to analyze the minutes of phone calls for thousands of virtual users, and get their bill according to their various phone plans. We had a chance to practise our knowledge in abstract classes and inheritance. Our second task, we created a tree map for files and subfolders in a given folder. It is fun to see how rectangles of different sizes and colors represents various files. My final grade for this course is A.

  3. MAT223H1 Linear Algebra I

    4. MAT223H1 Linear Algebra I

    Instructed by Professor Jiaqi Liu

    Linear Algebra was new for me, because I haven't explore concepts like span and matrixes in a detailed manner during high school. I imagined it to be really hard, but it turns out to be easier than I thought. Although I was kind of stuck when I encountered dimensions and eigenvectors, I managed to learn more in the study sessions before the midterms and exams. It really helped me to consolidate the knowledge, and to make sense of different modules when they all intercept with each other. My final grade for this course is A+.

  4. MUS202H1 Beethoven

    5. MUS202H1 Beethoven

    Instructed by Professor Remi Chiu

    To be frank, I took this course originally to satisfy my br requirements, and therefore I credit-no-credit it in the beginning. However, this course is more fascinating than I thought it would be.I did not only explored further in Beethoven's music, including his symphonies, his sonatas, but also get a chance to know more about his personal life and the political environment he was in. Towards the end of the course, my group did a group project on the Immortal Beloved, which is a series of love letters written by Beethoven to an unknown woman. I passed the course (no grade as I credit no credit it).
